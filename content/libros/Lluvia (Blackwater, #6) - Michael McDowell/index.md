    He was born in Enterprise, Alabama, in 1950 and died of AIDS-related illness in Boston, Massachusetts, in 1999.<br /><br
    />His first horror novel, <i>The Amulet</i>, relates the tragedies that befall various individuals who come in possession
    of a supernatural pendant in a small town. <br /><br />In McDowell's second novel, <i>Cold Moon Over Babylon</i>, a murdered
    woman's corpse is dispatched into a river, but her spirit roams the land, and in the evening hours it seeks revenge on
    her killer even as he plots the demise of her surviving relatives. <br /><br />Don D'Ammassa, writing in the <i>St. James
    Guide to Horror, Ghost &amp; Gothic Writers</i>, noted that McDowell's ability to maintain a sense of mundane normalcy
    against supernatural activity provides the novel with "a fine balance between reality and unreality," and he called <i>Cold
    Moon Over Babylon</i> "one of the best ghost stories ever written at novel length."<br /><br />A similarly disturbing
    tension between dull reality and the supernatural is produced in <i>The Elementals</i>, wherein a host of visitors come
    to stay at a secluded house occupied by embodiments of elemental forces. <br /><br />McDowell's <i>Katie</i>, meanwhile,
    concerns a clairvoyant serial killer whose powers of perception enable her to evade her trackers. The attractive but deranged
    heroine of this novel manages to conduct her murderous activities despite the awareness of her parents, who are content
    to derive financial gain from their daughter's crimes.<br /><br />Madness is central to McDowell's <i>Toplin</i>, which
    details the vile imaginings of a man who suffers from mental illness but nonetheless determines to conduct himself within
    society. D'Ammassa praised Toplin as "perhaps the best novel ever written from the point of view of a schizophrenic."<br
    /><br />Among McDowell's other writings is the six-part serial novel Blackwater, a chronicle of a southern family drawn
    to the supernatural. In addition, McDowell has also supplied the screenplays for various films, including director Tim
    Burton's horror comedy <i>Beetlejuice</i> and his animated production <i>The Nightmare Before Christmas</i>.<br /><br
    />Stephen King called McDowell one of the "finest writers of paperback originals in America today." Tabitha King was asked
    to complete McDowell's unfinished novel <i>Candles Burning</i>, which was published in 2006 to good reviews. Concerning
    his career, McDowell never tried to be something he wasn't. "I am a commercial writer and I'm proud of that", he said
    in the book <i>Faces of Fear</i> in 1985. "I am writing things to be put in the bookstore next month. I think it is a
    mistake to try to write for the ages."
